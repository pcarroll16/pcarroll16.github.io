Socioeconomic Effects of Air Quality Compliance: Milestone 1 Submission

By Peter Carroll

Project Overview: This project aims to investigate potential relationships between air qualityspecifically PM2.5 (Particulate Matter) levelsand employment metrics across U.S. states. The following data used are publicly available datasets from the EPA Air Quality System (AQS) and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). I will assess trends in both environmental and labor market data. We selected these datasets for their relevance, size, and public accessibility, with potential room for expansion to public health outcomes. The EPAs dataset provides granular PM2.5 measurements at the county level, while the BLS data provides state-level employment figures, including total employment and average annual wages across industries. While the datasets differ in spatial resolution (state vs. county), they offer an opportunity to explore side-by-side trends and insights in the exploratory phase of this project. If time permits, I would like to examine specific county level trends overtime. Furthermore, I would like to examine which specific industries lead to the most particulate matter emissions. However, for this milestone 1 submission, I decided to look at trends on the state level.

Initial research question: How do trends in fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentrations relate to economic indicators such as employment levels and average wages, speciifcally in manufacturing industries, across different U.S. states and c

Questions for each data set:

EPA AQS - PM2.5 Data (2018) Question: Which states have the highest and lowest average PM2.5 concentrations in 2018?

BLS State Occupational Employment and Wage Estimates (2018) Question: How do total employment and average wages vary by state and industry?

Question for merged data set: Are there noticeable trends/correlations between particulate matter emissions and industrial output?

# Clean and standardize death data
def clean_death_data(df, system_name):
    # Drop any rows with missing or non-numeric death counts
    df = df.copy()
    df = df[df['Deaths'].notna()]
    df = df[~df['Deaths'].isin(['Suppressed', 'Unreliable'])]

    # Convert columns to numeric
    df['Deaths'] = pd.to_numeric(df['Deaths'], errors='coerce')
    df['Population'] = pd.to_numeric(df['Population'], errors='coerce')
    df['Crude Rate'] = pd.to_numeric(df['Crude Rate'], errors='coerce')

    # Create label to distinguish death type
    df['System'] = system_name

    # Keep only needed columns
    df = df[['County Code', 'County', 'Year', 'Cause of death', 'Deaths', 'Population', 'Crude Rate', 'System']]
    return df

# Clean each dataset

resp_clean = clean_death_data(resp_df, 'Respiratory')
